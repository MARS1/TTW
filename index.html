Ticket to Work Site Re-Design & Logo Concept

December 26, 2013

Initial Summary:

The Ticket to Work Re-design revolves around the main recommendations on the Agenda (See executive summary page 1) that are as follows:

  • Recommendation 1: Make it easier to find information on the site
  • Recommndation 2: Make site mobile friendly
  • Recommendation 3: Bring new content front and center and align with choice messaging
  • Recommendation 4: Connect related content across the website
  • Recommendation 5: Build out themes and topics by Audience
  • Recommendation 6: Create a Unified Multimedia Gallery

The idea in the user experience is to make the content easier to find, what reamins to be clear to me is what that content is. I believe as the design progresses so must the exact type/class of content be clearly identified to help us holisitically understand why that information is important and to whom specifically from our key audiences does it benefit.

I believe i'm on the same page as the team when I say that the best user experience is serving up what the user needs and specifically what is relevant to them only. I attempt to briefly illustrate that in the following examples by introducing content based on introductory questions. This concept, while not fully fleshed out in the mockups, will engage our user to interact with their serach and won't obfuscate what they need to do or where they should begin to look.

Keeping choices to a minimal from the beginning and using cookies to track that users preferences are all part of larger role to keep the user on the right path and unecessarily overwhelemed.

Design and user experience cannot alone be the end all solution. Tracking the devices our users are accessing the site on, what pages they tend to stay on the longes or repetitively access, as well as the browwers they use are all part of an ongoing investigation that will guide us in how best to serve the end user. Google Analytics will play an increasingly important role when determinining these factors and I also think introducing new third party solutions (like Optimizely) will allow us to not feel like we have to marry any one concept and will be key in avoiding guesswork when making decisions like which homepage is the most effective, or which call-out received the most interaction, etc.

With this new re-design comes more than just a new look, but rather a new way to enhance how we perform business as usual by testing all that we do and allowing our analytics to guide our efforts in the most efficient manner possible.

Logo Concepts

Logo Concept 1:

The initial concept began off of the idea of a path or road to employment based on current concept ideas and print materials. I wanted to incorporate that in a clean and bold way while still creating a logo that worked on a wide range of marketing and promotional material and advertisement.

Logo Concept 2:

The Second concept was taking the path and making it more symetrical and possible to multi-position it (top, bottom, right, let, etc). At this point I was also exploring a couple different color schemes based on the notiion of those that are color-blind. Some research led me to understand that strong colors like green and red are difficult to view in some cases.

Logo Concept 3:

After framing the logo in a rectangle and keeping it without a boundary I started thinking about encompassing it in a circle and viewing it more like a sticker and stuck with that idea as an alternative for the logo, which is very common of logos to have a couply variants for different use cases.
